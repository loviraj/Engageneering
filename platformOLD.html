Think of the lecture as a recipe, not a monologue. Break every 10 minutes with a 2-minute "construction pause" — students don't review notes, they build something: a one-sentence explanation to a peer, a quick sketch of the concept, a real-world example they've encountered. The content coverage doesn't shrink; it gets processed rather than just received. The curriculum is still covered — but now it lands somewhere.
